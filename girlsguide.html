Girls guide

Girl Guides is a program for girls that offers a variety of activities to help them become confident young women. Girl Guides are organized into units or troops of 15â€“30 girls, led by a team of leaders. The units are further divided into patrols of about six girls. The name "Girl Guides" comes from the Corps of Guides, a regiment in the British Indian Army that was known for its tracking and survival skills. The World Association of Girl Guides and Girl Scouts (WAGGGS) is the world's largest voluntary movement for girls and young women. The term "Girl Scout" is used in the United States and several East Asian countries, while "Girl Guides" is used in other languages.
